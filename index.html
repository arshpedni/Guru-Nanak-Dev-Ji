🌅 Early Life & Key Events

This section details the formative years and pivotal moments of Guru Nanak's early life. Follow the timeline below to understand his origins and the events that sparked his spiritual revolution.

Origins

  • 📅
    Birth: April 15, 1469 (celebrated as Gurpurab in Kartik).
  • 📍
    Place: Rai Bhoi Ki Talwandi (now Nankana Sahib, Pakistan).
  • 👨‍👩‍👦
    Parents: Mehta Kalu (father) and Mata Tripta (mother).
  • ✨
    Early Signs: From childhood, showed deep interest in spiritual matters, questioning rituals and caste.

Age 11: The Sacred Thread

Refused to wear the ritualistic Janeu thread, arguing that a person should be defined by actions and mercy, not a string.

🍞 Sacha Sauda (True Bargain)

Given 20 rupees by his father for business. Instead, he used it to feed hungry wandering saints, defining this as the "True Bargain."

Sultanpur Lodhi

Worked as a storekeeper while living with his sister, Bebe Nanaki. Spent mornings meditating by the Kali Bein river.

The Divine Enlightenment

Disappeared for three days in the Kali Bein river. Emerged with a profound realization:

"Na Ko Hindu, Na Ko Musalman"
(There is no Hindu, there is no Muslim)

📖 Core Teachings

This section distills Guru Nanak's vast philosophy into its core components. Explore the "Three Pillars" of a truthful life and the fundamental concepts that form the basis of his worldview. Click on the pillars below for details.

The Three Pillars

1

Naam Japo

Meditation & Remembrance

Meditate on the name of God and remember Him at all times. It is a constant internal connection to the divine truth.
2

Kirat Karo

Honest Living

Earn an honest living through hard work and integrity. Spirituality is not an excuse to escape worldly responsibilities.
3

Vand Chakko

Sharing with Others

Share what you have with the needy and the community. True wealth is found in generosity and collective well-being.

Fundamental Concepts

ੴ

Ik Onkar

God is One. A unified, formless, and universal divine presence.

⚖️

Absolute Equality

Complete rejection of the caste system and gender discrimination.

🍲

Langar

The community kitchen where everyone sits on the floor together to eat, regardless of status.

🏡 Later Years & Legacy

This final section explores the culmination of Guru Nanak's earthly journey, the establishment of a localized community, and the lasting revolution of logic and love he left behind.

Kartarpur & Succession

  • 🌾

    Guru Nanak eventually settled down in Kartarpur (City of God) and lived as a farmer, demonstrating 'Kirat Karo' in practice.

  • 🤲

    He established a vibrant community of followers who lived a balanced life of deep prayer and honest manual labor.

  • 👑

    Succession: Before his passing, he tested his disciples and chose Bhai Lehna as his successor, bypassing his own sons. He renamed him Guru Angad Dev Ji (meaning "a part of one's own body").

  • ✨

    Joti Jot: He left his physical form in the year 1539.

🌿

Summary for Reflection

Guru Nanak Dev Ji's life was a revolution of love and logic.

He didn't ask people to renounce the world; he asked them to live in the world with a pure heart, serving humanity as a form of highest worship.
